Cradle to Cradle

Group Studio Project - Islamabad, 2017

This was a short group project that was part of Autodesk Cradle to Cradle Challange.

Cotton farming and textile production touches upon two of the largest contributors to the Pakistani economy. The textile industry and market consumption is vast, yet, unfortunately there is no culture of recycling and responsibly disposing of these cotton based textile products. Instead it is widely accepted notion in every household that eventually all discarded items will go in trash. There is simply no social understanding that all of this waste ends up going to landfills which are in abundance and full of plastic. This plastic prevents organic material like cotton from decomposing properly.

The second major issue with the cotton processing is the abundance of resources that are utilized which are hazardous and uneconomical. Studies have shown water wastage and contamination is one of largest issues as a result of these processes. Despite these existing problems, plans are being laid out to build even more textile factories on agricultural land in Faislabad, which is the central to the textile industry in Pakistan.

Our main goal for this challenge was to identify points of intervention which would enable us to prevent wastage of this material. The first step was of course to understand the way the agricultural industry and textile industry worked together.

The very first stage in cotton textile production deals with actual raw cotton which is taken from the fields, after harvest, to the gin where the crop is cleaned and separated into three parts. 10% of this cotton is discarded as trash. Modern gins can process up to 230,000KG in a day which means 23000KG of trash cotton goes to waste.

Our design solution aims at utilizing this ‘waste’ cotton to create a textile which consists of compressed fibers. This textile requires manufacturing processes which are readily available in the textile industry and it is cost effective and can decompose in 5 months.

Nurseries in Pakistan use plastic bags to cultivate plant saplings so they are easy to transport and replant. The sapling container created with this textile allows saplings to be directly planted into the soil along with the cotton bags without polluting the soil which the current plastic packaging enables.

The Seed Tape and the Seed Packets work on the same principle. They have plant seeds embedded within them which allows easier and efficient cultivation of plants as the seeds are evenly spaced out. This is a responsible zero waste system in itself and it prevents further waste from piling up the landfills. Currently seeds are packaged in plastic bags, however implementation of this kind of organic approach to agricultural tools can have very large scale impacts because of its relevance in the Pakistani context.
