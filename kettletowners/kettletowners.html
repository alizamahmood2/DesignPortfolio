Kettletowners

Group Project - Washington DC, May 2019

Americans + the Holocaust Game Design Residency



Led by: Colleen Macklin and John Sharp

USHMM Facilitator: Michael Goldman

In collaboration with the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum’s ((USHMM) Future Projects group, focusing on the ‘American + the Holocaust’ Exhibit.

We explored the potential of play and interactivity as support mechanisms for USHMM’s “Americans and the Holocaust” exhibit. The aim was to create interactive experiences that would make very difficult and emotional topics that the museum exhibits approachable enough for visitors to engage with and start a conversation.

The Residency was composed of three parts: an Immersive Visit, two Design Workshops, and a week-long Design Residency.

Our team chose to focus on a small overlooked part of the exhibit about the Southbury Bund conflict of 1937. This information is displayed in a transitory space of the exhibit that takes people from one section to another. Therefore, this part goes unseen in the exhibit as most people would just walk past it, without stopping to engage.

Below are some images of the exhibit.

‘Kettletowners’ is a multi-media roleplaying game designed to engage with this history of 1930's small town America's resistance to Nazi Bund camps occupying parts of their community. It is set in 1937, in a townhall meeting in Southbury. It was aimed to give players an opportunity to understand how and why decisions were made, and the impact that these decisions can have, to show how local, organized action can make change. We used phone polling software to see how people would drop in and out, and voice anonymous opinions.

A session of Kettletowners starts of with an orientation of the history that players will be addressing, an introduction from a town hall meeting moderator, and then a thrice-run cycle of issues, proposed solutions, and voting, to decide on the course of action.

Conditions and Materials:


o Players: 3 or more
o Time: 20 mins – 40 mins
o Phones with internet connection (for players)
o Laptop, with ‘Poll Everywhere’ downloaded
o Projector
o Speaker

We engaged with the USHMM staff and some visitors of the museum in the conceptualizing, designing, playtesting, and evaluation of game prototypes.

Made in collaboration with Waverly Wilson and Yufei Zhang during a multi-week residency at the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum.


Disclaimer: This game was created with the attempt of honoring, and memorializing the true stories of 1937 Southbury Connecticut’s actions. In no way do we consider this game to teach players the emotional experiences those real people experienced, but we hope this game can help people better understand and engage with their history.
