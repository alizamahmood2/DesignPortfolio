Lamp Object

Individual Elective Project - New York, 2019

Course: Computational Craft

I constructed this lamp using soft conductive materials, fabric and wood. It’s made up of cuboids and different parts come together to complete the circuit.

This lamp is made up of little cuboid frames of wood, with fabric on either of the larger sides. Each cuboid contains either a red, orange or yellow LED inside and has a different type and color of fabric attached, creating a diffusion library of sorts, when lit. They all have individual switches; each cuboid connects to the larger circuit through a snap button at the bottom, and by winding a piece of conductive thread on the nail at the top and then securing it down through the slit in the wood. Therefore, these modules can be replaced, lessened or rearranged to slightly alter the aesthetic of the light.

This object is a more embodied way of interaction because the typical way of use (the way you would connect everything to turn it on) allows you to explore the form in the process of doing so. What can be gained from having these ‘scripts’ of use overlap with ‘scripts’ of interacting with form?


The following images are taken in order of construction, to bring the whole form together:

The entire form acts as a tilt switch. When placed vertically, it turns off.

When placed horizontally, it is lit. There is a small nut at the back that makes contact and completes the circuit only when the object is horizontally placed. (refer to the last image)

Materials Used:


o Wood (basswood planks, 3/16″ thickness, cherry planks, 1/32″ thickness)
o Fabric - Some weaved, some knitted, in different colors and thicknesses.
o Conductive thread
o 10mm LEDs (2 red, 2 yellow, 3 orange)
o 3V battery (2)
o Conductive fabric
o Snap buttons (7)
o Nails (7) + hammer
o Nut (1)
o Coping saw + clamps (to cut wood)
o Thread and needle
o Fabric glue
o Tape
o Copper tape
o Super glue
o Hot glue
o Scissors
o Xacto knife
